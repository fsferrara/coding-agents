# Technical Documentation Checklist

Use this checklist before publishing, handing off, or completing a substantial documentation rewrite or review.

## Purpose and Audience

- Can you state the one question or job this document answers?
- Is the intended reader clear?
- Does the page lead with the answer, outcome, or purpose?
- Is the content useful to future readers, not only the current conversation?

## Placement and Hierarchy

- Is this the correct home for the content?
- Does an existing page already cover the topic?
- Is there one canonical source instead of several competing versions?
- Can readers find the page through navigation without relying only on search?
- Is the navigation order logical and intentional?
- Will the hierarchy still work as more content is added?
- Are secondary pages linking to the canonical source instead of duplicating it?

## Content Quality

- Is every instruction accurate today?
- Did you validate anything readers will copy, click, or trust?
- Are commands, paths, names, versions, and permissions exact?
- Are titles and headings specific and truthful?
- Are links current and useful?
- Did you remove outdated, duplicated, empty, or misleading content?
- Did you move durable knowledge out of comments, chat, tickets, or email?
- Did you include verification or troubleshooting where readers need it?
- Are examples neutral unless the document is about a specific vendor, product, or organization?

## Writing Quality

- Did you remove filler such as `please`, `simply`, `just`, `in order to`, `you can`, and `this document will explain`?
- Did you replace vague wording with exact names, paths, versions, commands, and page titles?
- Did you use one term consistently for each concept?
- Did you convert long paragraphs into lists where appropriate?
- Do action-oriented headings and list items start with verbs where possible?
- Did you prefer active voice, simple language, and direct phrasing?
- Did you avoid noun strings and unnecessary formality?

## Final Pass

- Does a scanner understand the document from headings, lists, commands, and examples?
- Does the document avoid long introductions before the useful answer?
- Does the document have no orphaned sections, empty headings, or stale template text?
- If headings changed, did you preserve or flag any anchors that inbound links may use?
